Keynote Speaker: David Pengelley
Professor, Department of Mathematical Sciences, New Mexico State University


Title: "Voici ce que j'ai trouve (Here is what I have found):
Sophie Germain's rediscovered manuscripts on Fermat's Last Theorem"


Abstract: Sophie Germain is best known in number theory
for the first general result aimed at proving Fermat's Last Theorem.
However, her unpublished manuscripts, and a letter to Gauss, reveal that,
for her, this result was only minor fallout from a grand plan she pursued
for proving the theorem outright. The scope of Germain's attempt remained
unequaled for half a century, until her path was unknowingly trodden again
by others. We will explore her grand plan and its side thrusts, including
remarkable lower bounds on the size of possible solutions to Fermat's equation.
